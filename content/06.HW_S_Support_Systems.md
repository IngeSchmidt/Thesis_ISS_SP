# Support Systems
Next to the large modules that are included in the project, there are also smaller modules that are used to support the main modules. These support systems are crucial for the overall functionality and performance of the project. In this section, multiple support systems will be discussed on their the design, evaluation, and testing of these systems. The support systems that will be discussed include the following:
1. Audio output
2. Power supply
3. Selectable input sources
4. Playing guide
5. Housing

## Audio output
### Design description
The sound sculpture would be incomplete without a way to hear your own music. To ensure that the output is as close to the sound that is played, it is important to us a good speaker. It is also important that the speaker is loud enough to play in a classroom.  The Raspberry Pi has a headphone jack with a high output impedance. To drive a speaker, an amplifier must be used to supply enough power.

To choose the right amplifier the trade-off between power consumption and quality must be analysed. To keep the development simple, the power consumption must be low, as a high consumption prompts the cooling of the amplifier. A Class-D amplifier uses a square wave that is manipulated to control the sound. This makes it possible to reach high efficiencies. A suitable amplifier is the “PAM8302”. Adafruit made a module to simplify control the amplifier which is used in this project. [@tuite_understanding_amplifier_operating_classes]

### Evaluation and testing methods
The relevant requirements from the programme of requirements are:
1.	The device must be able to adjust the volume from 0 to 100 percent of the amplifier’s maximum output.
2.	The speaker must produce a hearable sound that does not exceed 85 dB measured at a distance of 1 meter.
3.	The device must be electrically insulated and comply with standard safety regulations to ensure the user is protected from high-voltage hazards.

The input conrol will digitally control the volume of the amplifier. The volume will be controlled by a potentiometer that is connected to the ESP32. The volume will be adjusted from 0 to 100 percent of the amplifier’s maximum output.

#### Maximum volume and current test
The maximum volume test is designed to test the volume and current of all waveforms when the volume input is at 100% and the frequency is at 1520 Hz (as the datasheet for the speaker gives this as highest frequency response). Next to safety, the maximum current is also important to monitor the current distribution in the system.
Test setup:
1.	Take a (sound) decibel meter and place it 1 meter from the speaker.
2.	Install a current measuring at the VCC of the amplifier module (wire multimeter on current mode in series) 
3.	Turn on the system and tune the volume to 100%.
4.	Play all waveforms at 1520Hz, register the measures volumes and currents.

### Testing results and discussion
The maximum voltage and current test yielded the following result: 
| Waveform         | Volume [dB] | Current [mA] |
|------------------|------------:|-------------:|
| Sine wave        | 71.8        | 15.23        |
| Triangular wave  | 75.8        | 12.12        |
| Rectangular wave | 77.1        | 22.26        |
| No input         | 30.5        | 6.66         |

### Conclusion
The volume of all waves does not exceed 85 dB. Therefore the system complies with the first requirement. The current in combination with the maximum 5 volts is safe to work with, as the 
[Low Voltage Directive (LVD)](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/electrical-and-electronic-engineering-industries-eei/low-voltage-directive-lvd_en) 
only starts from 75 Volts DC and 50 AC.
